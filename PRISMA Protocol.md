# PRISMA Protocol  
Hybrid Augmented Intelligence in the Vehicle Routing Problem (2015–2025)

---

## 1. Research Objective

This systematic review investigates the integration of:

- Human expertise
- Metaheuristics and hyper-heuristics
- Machine learning
- Fuzzy logic
- Multi-criteria decision-making (MCDM)
- Decision-support systems

within the Vehicle Routing Problem (VRP).

The review aims to determine whether the literature reflects genuine Augmented Intelligence systems (Human + AI), or primarily algorithmic hybridization (AI + AI).

---

## 2. Research Questions

RQ1: How are humans integrated into VRP optimization frameworks?  
RQ2: How are rules, preferences, ML, fuzzy systems, and metaheuristics combined?  
RQ3: What decision-support architectures exist in VRP systems?  
RQ4: What advances have been achieved between 2015–2025?  
RQ5: Where are the gaps in cognitive integration?

---

## 3. Data Source

Database: OpenAlex

Three structured searches were conducted.

---

## 4. Search Strategy

### Research 01 – Algorithmic Baseline

("Vehicle Routing Problem" OR "VRP")  
AND  
("heuristic" OR "metaheuristic" OR "hyper-heuristic")

Purpose: Establish algorithmic landscape.

---

### Research 02 – Hybrid AI + Decision Support

VRP-related terms  
AND  
Decision-support / interactive optimization terms  
AND  
Hybrid AI / ML / fuzzy / metaheuristic terms

Purpose: Capture hybrid and decision-integrated systems.

---

### Research 03 – Human-Centered Optimization

VRP-related terms  
AND  
("interactive" OR "decision maker" OR "human in the loop" OR "preference elicitation" OR "participatory")

Purpose: Isolate explicit human-AI collaboration systems.

---

## 5. Inclusion Criteria

- Peer-reviewed articles
- Published between 2015–2025
- Explicitly addressing VRP or routing optimization
- Involving at least one of:
  - Metaheuristic/hyper-heuristic
  - ML-guided optimization
  - Fuzzy modeling
  - MCDM
  - Decision-support systems
  - Human-in-the-loop systems

---

## 6. Exclusion Criteria

- Purely theoretical VRP variants without decision integration
- Non-routing logistics papers
- Non-peer-reviewed documents
- Papers without algorithmic or decision components

---

## 7. Screening Process

1. Identification (database extraction)
2. Duplicate removal
3. Title screening
4. Abstract screening
5. Full-text eligibility
6. Final inclusion

---

## 8. Data Extraction Fields

For each included paper:

- Year
- Journal
- Method type
- Human integration type
- ML role
- Fuzzy role
- Metaheuristic used
- Decision framework type
- Validation (benchmark / real case)
- Industry application (if any)

---

## 9. Expected Outcome

The review will produce:

- A taxonomy of Hybrid Augmented Intelligence in VRP
- A classification of decision integration levels
- Identification of research gaps
- A future research agenda
